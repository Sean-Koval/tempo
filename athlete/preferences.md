# Coaching Preferences & Constraints

How Sean wants to be coached, and what constraints the coach should always respect.

## Coaching style

- Prefer **structured periodization** (phase-based) with **LLM judgment for adjustments** — not pure rule-following, not pure vibes.
- Explain the *why* of every meaningful adjustment. Rationale beats authority.
- Bias toward **consistency over hero sessions**. Missed workouts > completed-but-in-the-hole workouts.
- Be conservative with intensity progressions and aggressive with easy-day compliance.

## Schedule & logistics

Current framework (cycling-led, run paused for left-tibia BSI recovery — see `injury-log.md`):

- **Training days/week:** 6 (1 full rest day, rotating; typically the day after the hardest bike + strength stack).
- **Weekly hour cap:** building from ~9 hrs → ~12 hrs over the next 8 weeks. Cycling 6 → 9+ hrs; strength ~2.5 hrs (3×45 min full + 1×20 min mini); rest of capacity is recovery.
- **Long-session days:** Saturday (long aerobic ride, scaling 90 min → 4 hr), Sunday (recovery spin).
- **Hard-day pattern:** Tue (hard bike) + Thu (hard bike). No back-to-back hard cycling.
- **Strength touches/week:** 4 (Mon Day-A lower, Wed Day-B upper, Fri Day-C hip/cycling-power, Tue evening Day-D mini upper).
- **Sport priority right now:** Bike (primary) → Strength (secondary, supporting cycling + tibia rehab) → Run (paused, BSI). Swim deferred until pool access + base built.
- **Sleep window:** _(fill in — drives next-day intensity gating)_
- **Daily readiness gate:** if HRV trending down 3+ days **and** TSB < −20, cut next hard session by default (see `decision-rules.md` R-1, R-3).

## Hard constraints

- Respect `injury-log.md` active flags **without exception**. Active: left-tibia BSI recovery — no running, calf-loading progressions tracked weekly.
- If HRV is trending down 3+ days **and** TSB < −20, cut the next hard session by default.
- Long-run progression max +10%/week by duration. **N/A while BSI active.**
- Never schedule the hardest bike session and hardest run session on consecutive days. **N/A while running paused.**
- Never schedule two hard cycling sessions back-to-back (preserves recovery between Tue/Thu hard days).
- Strength Day A (single-leg, calf-loading) does not stack same-day with any high-impact return-to-run progression.

## Soft preferences

- Favor outdoor long sessions on weekends; trainer OK midweek.
- Running: Z2 by HR drift, not pace. Heat adjusts the pace, not the physiology. **(Re-applies post-BSI.)**
- Swim: prioritize technique blocks when CTL ramp > 5/week (fatigue hurts stroke quality). **(Re-applies once swimming starts.)**
- Cycling cadence: keep neuromuscular work (S4) every 1–2 weeks to maintain pedal smoothness even in build phases.
