# Daily fueling

## Macros (defaults for a ~75kg endurance athlete)

- **Carbohydrate:** periodized, 5–12 g/kg/day depending on training load.
  - Easy/rest day: 3–5 g/kg
  - Moderate day (single aerobic session): 5–7 g/kg
  - Hard or long day: 7–10 g/kg
  - Race week: 8–12 g/kg
- **Protein:** 1.6–2.0 g/kg/day, distributed 4–5× 30–40g meals.
- **Fat:** remainder; not restricted, but not loaded either.

## Timing

- **Pre-session:** 1–2g carbs/kg 1–2h before, lower fat/fiber to aid gastric clearance.
- **During:** see `session-fueling.md`.
- **Post-session:** 1–1.2g carbs/kg + 25–40g protein within 60min, especially after long or hard sessions.

## Hydration

- Daily baseline: ~35 ml/kg + 500ml per hour of training.
- Sodium: daily ~2–3g; more in heat or heavy sweaters (confirm with sweat test).

## Supplements (evidence-based)

- **Caffeine** — 3–6 mg/kg 45–60min pre-race; train gut tolerance.
- **Creatine** — 3–5g/day; modest benefit to muscular endurance and recovery.
- **Vitamin D** — check levels; supplement if < 30 ng/ml.
- **Iron** — athletes (especially heavy training) check ferritin periodically; supplement only if low.
