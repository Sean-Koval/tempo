# Race-week carbohydrate loading

Goal: top up muscle and liver glycogen so race-morning starts with a full
tank. For a full Ironman, glycogen depletion is one of the top-3 finish-line
limiters; race-week loading is the cheapest performance gain available.

`knowledge/nutrition/athlete-tested.yaml` is the authority on what works for
Sean specifically. Use these numbers as defaults, not prescriptions.

## The math (for a 75 kg athlete)

| Day | Carbs (g/kg) | Total carbs | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Race-day -3 | 7 | ~525 g | Normal training intake. Light session OK. |
| Race-day -2 | 10 | ~750 g | Glycogen-loading day proper. Reduce activity to <45 min easy. |
| Race-day -1 | 10 | ~750 g | Continue load. Final shakeout < 30 min. Drop fiber. |
| Race morning | ~2 g/kg pre-start | ~150 g | 3–3.5 hr pre-race breakfast. |

Across the 3 days, that's roughly **30 g/kg total carb** above maintenance.
Body composition will shift up 1.5–2 kg from glycogen + bound water — that
is the protocol working, not weight gain.

## Food choices that scale

Hitting 750 g of carbs/day is a logistics problem more than a hunger
problem. Use carb-dense, low-volume, low-fiber foods:

- **Bases:** white rice, white pasta, sourdough/baguette, gnocchi, rice cakes.
- **Liquids carry surprising volume:** 500 ml fruit juice ≈ 60 g carbs.
  Sports drinks at 6% concentration ≈ 60 g/L. A second daily bottle of carb
  drink is the single easiest way to add 100+ g.
- **Sweet and bland combos:** white rice + honey, sourdough + jam, rice
  cake + maple syrup. Race-week is not the week for fiber-rich whole grains
  or raw vegetables.
- **Protein:** keep at maintenance (~1.6 g/kg). Don't displace carbs with
  protein-heavy meals.
- **Fat:** moderate — fat slows gastric emptying and crowds out carb space.

## Hydration & sodium during loading

- Fluid: ~40 ml/kg/day baseline + extra for any training. Pee should be pale
  yellow.
- Sodium: target 5–7 g/day across loading. Salt food generously; add a
  sodium-rich electrolyte drink between meals if normal diet is low-salt.
  This counters the "flat / heavy" feeling some athletes report on Day 2.

## Common mistakes

- **Loading on top of normal fiber.** A high-fiber gut + 750 g carbs = race-
  morning GI event. Drop fiber from Day -2 onward (no oats, no bran, no raw
  vegetables, peeled fruits only).
- **New foods.** Race week is not a tasting menu. Every food on the loading
  plan should have been eaten across at least one prior race-sim weekend.
- **Underestimating volume.** 750 g carbs ≈ 3000 kcal from carbs alone.
  Set 5 eating windows, not 3.
- **Skipping loading because "I'm tapered, I won't burn it."** Glycogen
  storage is bounded; the goal is *maximize* the start-line tank, not match
  expected expenditure.

## How this applies to a full Ironman race week

- Last hard session is Race-day -7 to -10. By Race-day -5 the body is
  glycogen-supercompensating naturally with normal carb intake.
- True loading window: Day -2 and Day -1.
- Race-morning breakfast is its own protocol — see `race-day.md`.

## Caveats

- Athletes with a history of GI distress at race-day may benefit from a
  shorter, gentler load (8 g/kg × 2 days) rather than the aggressive
  10 g/kg × 2.
- Heat changes nothing about carb need but increases fluid + sodium need.
- For half-distance and shorter races (<3 hr expected duration), the loading
  protocol is overkill — Day -1 alone at 8 g/kg is enough.
