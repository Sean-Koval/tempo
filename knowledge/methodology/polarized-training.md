# Polarized training — working notes

## Core claim

Most training time (~80%) at Z1–Z2 (below aerobic threshold, LT1) and a smaller but non-trivial share (~15–20%) at Z4–Z5 (above LT2). The Z3 "tempo trap" is minimized because it accumulates fatigue without a commensurate adaptation signal.

## How Tempo applies it

- **Base phase:** 85/10/5 (Z1-Z2 / Z3 / Z4+). Bias heavily toward durability-building Z2.
- **Build phase:** 75/15/10. Race-specific work moves into Z3–Z4.
- **Peak phase:** 75/15/10. Similar distribution; more race-specific structure.
- **Taper:** 80/15/5 — volume drops, intensity exposure preserved.

## Session implications

- Z2 sessions are measured by HR *drift*, not pace. Heat and fatigue move pace; physiology is still Z2 if drift < 5%.
- Cruise intervals (Z4, sustainable) land in the "high intensity" bucket — do not count them as Z3.
- Tempo bike blocks live at Z3 and are prescribed sparingly to avoid over-indexing on the middle zone.
